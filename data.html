The Data of World Weather

Conquest and challenge

This table is the information gathered from roughly 250 randomly selected cities courtesy of openweathermap.org and the CityPy, python library.

The purpose of compiling and using the data was to challenge and hopefully prove the widely held assumption that temperatures rise with proximity to the equator.

The key takeaway is that more analysis is needed because the data indicates a possibility that the equator may not be the hottest place on the globe. Evidence suggests that the southern hemisphere may be home to the most extreme temperatures on the -23° parallel. However, this is not conclusive, since the temperature readings from the data set may be random outliers and may be influenced by other factors. In fact, the vast majority of temperature readings do seem to support the concept it is indeed hotter the closer we are to 0° latitude.

The greatest challenge to the analysis are the gaps in the data. For one thing, the analysis is based on one snapshot in time: January 1, 2019. In seasonal terms, the two hemispheres are diametrically opposed to one another. It is winter in the north and summer in the south. It is possible that the more extreme southern temperatures simply reflect a temporary and seasonal spike. One wonders if a comparison between spring and fall would be less so. To reach a confident conclusion, weekly, monthly and yearly averages for all data points are needed. Even for the one day of data that is shown in the table below, more cities could have been included if fields of information that were missing had been replaced with acurate readings. Even so, it's hard to say that the additional cities would have much bearing on the outcome since many are close enough to one another that the weather data might not vary significantly. Additionally, it is impossible to glean equal numbers of city data for the southern hemisphere since only 10% of the world lives there. Simply put, there will always be more information available for the northern regions.

Since the purpose of this website project is to illustrate skills of visualization and not an in depth analysis, more data would be needed to reach a conclusion, but what we do know from the analysis so far, is that challenging the assumption of the equator being the hottest place on earth, is a worthy pursuit.
